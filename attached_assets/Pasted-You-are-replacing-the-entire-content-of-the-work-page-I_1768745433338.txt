Work That Shaped How I See Problems (Deep Dives)

Loanwiser — Fintech for Bharat (Associate Product Manager)

Loanwiser wasn’t a lending marketplace problem.  
It was a **decision confidence problem**.

Banks didn’t lack leads.
They lacked trust in lead quality, eligibility, and intent — which forced heavy manual verification and slowed disbursements.

What we built was not “just integrations.”

We created a decision layer between borrower and lender by:
- deeply analysing bank statements to understand income stability, not just balance
- combining credit score with behavioural signals
- generating lender-specific eligibility and risk reports
- routing users only to lenders where disbursement probability was genuinely high

The non-obvious insight:
Banks don’t want more leads — they want **fewer, better decisions**.

Impact:
- Disbursement probability increased by ~90%
- Manual intervention from banks reduced significantly
- Trust improved on both sides of the marketplace

This experience taught me how products fail quietly when they push decision-making downstream instead of owning it.

CarInfo / AppFlix — Monetising Without Breaking Trust (Senior Product Manager)

CarInfo already had scale.
What it lacked was **structured intent capture**.

Insurance was present, but the system treated all users the same — browsing users, curious users, and high-intent users were forced through identical flows.

The real problem wasn’t conversion.
It was **misaligned friction**.

What we changed:
- Rebuilt journeys based on real intent signals, not demographics
- Fixed quote → redirect → purchase drop-offs by rethinking sequencing, not copy
- Introduced context-aware nudges instead of aggressive CTAs
- Treated partner selection as a product problem, not a business one

Results:
- Bike insurance scaled from ~10/day to 6,800+ per day
- Car insurance scaled from ~5/day to 2,300+ per day
- CAC reduced by 25–30%
- Core engagement remained intact

The non-obvious insight:
Monetisation breaks when product teams chase revenue before understanding *why users are ready to pay*.
