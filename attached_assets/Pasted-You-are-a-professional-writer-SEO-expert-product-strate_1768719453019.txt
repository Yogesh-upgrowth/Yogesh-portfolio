You are a professional writer, SEO expert, product strategist, and front-end developer.  
Your task is to generate a **complete, high-engagement blog system** for Yogesh Yadav’s website.

GOALS:
1. Generate **50 unique blog articles** (10 in each of 5 categories).
2. Each article must be **min 2000 words**.
3. Each article must be **engaging, well-structured**, and written in **Yogesh Yadav’s professional voice** (thoughtful, practical, experience-driven).
4. Each article must use a **unique relevant featured image** (not the same image).
5. The output must include **full HTML structure + content** for each article.
6. The UI/formatting should follow the **Neil Patel blog style** (use clear sections, hooks, TOC, subheads, callouts, practical examples, lists, and strong CTAs).
7. Add SEO elements: meta title, meta description, appropriate tags, heading hierarchy (H1, H2, H3).

CONTENT RULES:
- Do NOT reuse or repeat images or content.
- Do NOT use placeholders like “Image here”.
- Each article must feel like a **signature piece** written to educate, engage, and persuade readers, with insights, examples, and actionable takeaways.
- Do not make up data without explanation — use logical reasoning, hypothetical or general context relevant to product/growth.
- Write each article as if the reader wants *practical value, not fluff*.

CATEGORIES (create exactly these 5):

1. AI Update  
2. Product Resources  
3. Market Research  
4. Product Knowledge  
5. Leadership

Each category must have **10 differentiated article topics** (define titles, descriptions, images, tags).

For each article:

SECTION 1 — META & OPENING
- Meta title (unique and SEO-focused)
- Meta description (optimised for clicks)
- Open with a strong **hook** (story, question, surprising insight)
- State what the article covers
- Reading time estimate

SECTION 2 — TABLE OF CONTENTS (autoinserted with anchor links)

SECTION 3 — ARTICLE BODY
Use this structure:

**Intro**
- Engaging narrative
- Human example, scenario, or problem

**Core Sections**
Write logically flowing sections with:
- Subheadings (H2, H3)
- Short paragraphs
- Bullet lists
- Numbered lists
- Examples
- Callout boxes (e.g., “Pro Tip”, “Common Mistakes”, “Data Insight”)

**Data & Authority**
- Include sourced logic, broad industry context (but do not copy from other sites)

**Visual Breaks**
- Suggest places where graphs, icons, or annotated visuals would go (named appropriately)

**Conclusion / Key Takeaways**
- 5–7 summarised points
- Practical checklist if relevant

**Strong CTA**
End with an engaging CTA oriented toward product leaders and founders:
“If this resonated, here’s how to work with me… / Contact Yogesh Yadav.”

IMAGES
- Generate a clear description for each featured image (1500x900) relevant to the topic.
- Provide ALT text describing the image.

UI REQUIREMENTS (HTML)
- Responsive HTML
- Semantic tags (article, section, header, footer)
- Human-readable, not minified
- Comments where necessary
- Follow Neil Patel’s blog styling cues (clear spacing, readable fonts, visual breaks)
- Include at least one internal link to other related blog posts where natural
- Include tags (SEO & UX)

OUTPUT STRUCTURE:
- Blog home page with categories and article cards
- Individual article pages (full content)
- Each article page must contain:
   • Unique featured image
   • Meta tags
   • Table of Contents
   • Well-structured body
   • Conclusion + key takeaways
   • Engaging CTA

DO NOT:
- Use the **same image** for every article
- Produce shallow or repetitive content
- Generate generic filler text
- Multiply exact sentences or headlines
- Use placeholders like “tbd”

Write everything in a **single output**, well-commented, clean, and ready to deploy as HTML + CSS.
