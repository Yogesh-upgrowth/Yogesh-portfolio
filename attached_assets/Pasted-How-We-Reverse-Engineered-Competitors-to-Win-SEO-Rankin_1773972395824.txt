Step 1: Deconstructing Competitor SEO Systems

Instead of guessing, we broke competitors down like products.

We analyzed 3 layers:
Layer 1: Keyword Architecture

We mapped:

All ranking keywords

Search volume

Intent type (informational vs transactional)

What we discovered:

Competitors didn’t target keywords randomly.

They structured them like this:

Layer	Example	Intent
Top Funnel	“How to send money abroad”	Awareness
Mid Funnel	“Best money transfer services India to UK”	Consideration
Bottom Funnel	“Send money to UK from India”	Decision

👉 They owned the entire funnel

Layer 2: Page Type System

We identified 5 core page types:

Country corridors (India → UK, India → Canada)

Comparison pages

Provider pages

Guides

Tools/calculators

But here’s the insight:

👉 Every page was designed to push toward comparison

Not just inform.

Layer 3: Internal Linking Graph

This was the hidden weapon.

Every guide linked to comparison pages

Every comparison page linked to providers

Every provider linked back to corridors

👉 A tightly connected SEO mesh

The Insight That Changed Everything

SEO is not pages.
SEO is a demand capture system.

And competitors had built:

Entry points (content)

Decision engines (comparison pages)

Conversion flows (CTAs)

Step 2: Designing Our SEO System (Not Strategy)

We didn’t copy competitors.

We built a more structured system:

The MoneyRateFinder SEO Engine

We broke it into 4 core systems:

1. Demand Mapping Engine

Example Journey:

“best transfer apps India to Canada”

“cheap remittance Canada”

Entry queries

Comparison queries

👉 1 cluster = 1 ecosystem

Each page dynamically pulled:

Exchange rates

Impact

3. Comparison-First UX System

This was our biggest differentiator.

Competitors:

Content → CTA

Page Structure Shift

Old:

Users don’t want to read.
