# 🧠 How We Used ML to Predict Insurance Buyers (50,000+ Data Points)

### Building a Real-Time Intent Engine for Conversion Optimization

---

## 🧲 Hook: The Problem Hidden in Plain Sight

> We analyzed over **50,000+ user journeys** to answer one simple but critical question:
>
> **“Which users are actually going to buy insurance — and when?”**

At first glance, everything looked fine.

* Millions of users
* High engagement
* Strong traffic inflow

But one metric told a very different story:

👉 **Insurance conversions were disproportionately low.**

Despite having users with:

* Relevant use cases
* Real-time triggers (expiry, RC checks)
* Clear monetization opportunity

We were still **failing to convert at scale**.

This wasn’t just a funnel problem.
It was a **system-level inefficiency**.

---

## 👤 Context: The Ecosystem We Were Operating In

The platform (Vehicle Info ecosystem) served users who came with a clear intent:

* Checking RC details
* Verifying ownership
* Reviewing insurance validity

These are **high-intent, real-world users**, not passive browsers.

👉 Which makes them extremely valuable from a monetization standpoint.

---

### ⚠️ The Disconnect

Even with strong relevance:

* Insurance modules were visible
* Timing seemed logical
* Traffic was high

👉 Yet, **conversion didn’t scale proportionally.**

---

## 🚨 Problem Statement

We were treating every user the same.

Every user:

* Saw the same insurance CTA
* Received similar nudges
* Experienced identical flows

---

### 📉 Observations

* Majority of users had **zero purchase intent**
* A small segment had **high intent**
* Most users dropped off early

---

### ⚠️ Core Issues

#### 1. No Intent Differentiation

We couldn’t distinguish between:

* Casual users
* Curious users
* Ready-to-buy users

---

#### 2. Generic User Experience

A user with expiring insurance
and a user casually browsing
→ received the same experience

---

#### 3. Lost High-Intent Users

Even ready-to-convert users:

* Faced friction
* Weren’t prioritized

---

### 💡 Core Realization

> We didn’t have a traffic problem.
> We had an **intent understanding problem**.

---

## 🔍 Key Insight: Intent is Behavioral

We observed that:

> Users don’t randomly convert —
> they exhibit **predictable behavioral signals** beforehand.

---

### 🧠 High-Intent Patterns

* Multiple searches
* Longer session duration
* Closer to expiry
* Repeated engagement

---

### ❄️ Low-Intent Patterns

* Single interaction
* Quick exit
* No engagement

---

### 💡 Breakthrough

> Intent is not declared — it is **inferred from behavior**.

---

## 🧠 Hypothesis

> If we can predict user intent using behavioral data,
> we can personalize their journey and increase conversions.

---

## 🗂️ Data Strategy (50K+ Users)

We analyzed **50,000+ user sessions**.

---

### 📊 Data Collected

#### Behavioral Signals

* Search count
* Session duration
* Scroll depth

#### Interaction Signals

* CTA clicks
* Insurance page time

#### Contextual Signals

* Days to expiry
* Vehicle age

#### Engagement Signals

* Notification interaction
* Return frequency

---

### 🧩 Feature Engineering

Each user → feature vector:

```
[Search_Count, Session_Time, Expiry_Days, CTA_Clicks, Return_Frequency]
```

---

### Derived Features

* Recency score
* Urgency score
* Engagement index
* Intent velocity

---

## ⚙️ ML Model & System

---

### Model Choice

* Logistic Regression (baseline)
* Gradient Boosting (XGBoost-style)

---

### Why These?

* Best for structured data
* Interpretable
* Scalable

---

### Output

👉 **Intent Score (0–100)**

---

### Segmentation

* 0–30 → Low Intent
* 30–70 → Medium Intent
* 70–100 → High Intent

---

## 🧩 System Architecture

```
User Action → Data Capture → Feature Processing → ML Model → Intent Score → Decision Engine → Personalized Experience
```
